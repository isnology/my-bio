The IBM I is a very different programming environment to web development. Most of the terms and technical aspects will mean nothing to a non IBM I developer, but are given here as an example of my coding.

The RPG language comes in 2 flavours:- the older RPG and the newer RPGLE. The "LE" on the end of RPG is short for "ILE" and stands for "Intergrated Language Environment", meaning that modules can be written in languages like C, Cobol, RPGLE or many others and they all pass parameters in a compatible way so that, say, an RPGLE can call a C module. In this intergrated environment there are static modues (like in C) and service programs (like a DLL in windows). Service programs are dynamicly linked at run time where as modules are linked in at compile time. I have not included the file definitions or external copy members (similar to header files in C).
